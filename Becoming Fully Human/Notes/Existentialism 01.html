Poem Notes: The Path That Leads to Nowhere By Corinne Roosevelt Robinson.

The author might be suggesting that the life lived where there is no path can also be good. Despite the path having no end and no start, the journey matters more. The "beauty of the dell" or the "marigolds amid the marshes". There is a lot of beauty in something that seems meaningless.

Furthermore, the writer seems to see the path that "do lead to Somewhere" is always rushing. It's like the writer is telling us to enjoy life for the moment we have instead of chasing something that might never come true. Being on the path of nowhere may still lead to somewhere and for this person they've found their soul.
