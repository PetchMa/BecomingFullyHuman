Write Up üìù

Intro

Personally, I've grown up weak and nimble my entire life. No I wasn't encumbered by medical problems or illness, but I was always below average. While I was at the school yard, I'd always be the smallest, the slowest, the scrawniest kid in the grade. I never really cared about my physical self, not even when I was bullied and shoved to the ground by bigger kids in my grade. My perspective never really changed until I saw a limitation within physical myself.

Over the summer I went on a trip to Mexico. On my first day there, things went south. Because of the exhausting flight coupled with the thick 35c + 80% humidity, my body began deteriorating in the heat. I was sweating bullets, and to make it worse, I was stuck on a trail in the jungle with no water. Long story short, I collapsed, and was unable to move my legs as I sat under a palm tree trying to keep myself from passing out. You might be thinking, "where are my parents??" Well... they were fine. They chugged on with barely any water left, but because of my weak body, I was limited by my physical abilities.

Thankfully, fellow hikers spared me their water and candies [electrolytes] and we decided to abandon that hike. As I sat on the car ride back, I just couldn't get over the fact that I missed out on a potentially amazing experience just because of my physical limitations. I don't want to live a life where my experiences are handicap by something that I can easily change. Thus when I got back I threw myself into improving and shaping a better physical self.

First reflection

I was determined to not let my body limit me. I was given such a healthy life I should take care of it. Thus I began doing exercises and workouts. After slowly building up my physical capabilities for the past 5 months, what stuck with me this time was the ability to overcome physical pain.

Humans are such weird creatures in a sense that we willing endure these types of pain to better ourselves in the long run. This ability is a muscle that I have yet to learn how to stretch. This is different to doing a difficult math problem or figuring out tough computer code, because this pain is real. I realized that the saying "no pain no gain" is true in many aspects of my life. To do anything great within my existence, I need to be willing accepting of the pain that comes with my journey towards my goals.

For example, I know I wish to pursue research in academic physics and mathematics. I am aware that there's a tremendous amount of hardship that is required to get me there. I've learned to be okay with that. A part of that acceptance is a translation of what I've learned from working out. There's moments of intense "pain" (pain as in burning pain from exhaustion) that I've learned to accept and love. This helped me develop a strong work ethic and stamina to do the things I love despite the great personal sacrifices I must make along the way.

Second Reflection

Following that, I've also recently began rock climbing and bouldering. Similar to working out, this is a sport that's all about pushing personal limits. You are given a wall, a cliff or a mountain and told to ascend to the summit or to top the route. There is something metaphoric about it, a great deal with climbing is the joy that comes with tackling the hardest problems you can put your body through and to better yourself with each route you top. In rock climbing, there's a "let's try it out" mentality where I've learned the confidence to just "give things a shot". This mentality has given me a very colorful life, a life with unique experiences, that's made memories that I can look back too. For example, a few weeks ago, a few of my friends just got on a bus at 2:30 am to Montreal alone and attempted a university coding competition for fun. We actually won an award... that was meant for the students at McGill. It wasn't intentional, we came to this opportunity thinking "what would happen if ..." that became a "lets give it a shot" which ended up being a really cool experience.

These are the philosophies I've learned while building my best physical self. These new perspectives were directly influenced by what I've accomplished with my body.
