Here I document some ideas related my body and how its shaped my life. There are many things I'm working towards and many things relating to how my body that has altered my perspective on life.

Personal Thoughts. [rough]

Personally I've never really grown up being spectacular in sports. I was always that typical nerdy kid that loved to do math and painted pictures. I wasn't physically inclined to do anything great in the physical aspect. It shouldn't be surprising that I was physically weak and small. Although this isn't a problem on its own, I quickly realized that I was limited by my physical capabilities. There was many things in life that I wished to do yet I was held back. A prime example of that was when I went to Mexico where, due to the exhausting heat, the laborious hike in the national park, I succumbed to my weakness having gotten a heat stroke in the middle of a jungle in Mexico. Not the greatest situation I was thankfully aided by a few passerby with some water and candy [for electrolytes]. I painstakingly abandoned the trip that day with my family and headed home.

After when I came home I was determined to not let my body limit me. I was given such a healthy life I should take care of it. Because of my previously limited physical capabilities I've found ways to work on myself. This evolved into me doing exercise and workouts. What stuck with me this time was the ability to overcome physical pain. Humans are such weird creatures in a sense that we willing endure these types of pain to better ourselves. This ability is a muscle that I have learned to stretch. I realized that the saying "no pain no gain" is true in many aspects of my life. To do anything great within my existence, I need to be willing accepting of the pain that comes with my journey towards my goals. For example, I know I wish to pursue research in academic physics and mathematics in post secondary but I know the tremendous amount of hardship that is required to get me there. Yet I've learned to be okay with that. A part of that acceptance is a translation of what I've learned from working out. There's moments of intense "pain" (pain as in burning pain from exhaustion). This helped me develop a strong work ethic and stamina to do the things I love despite the great personal sacrifices I must make along the way.

This personal training transitioned into a passion in bouldering/rock climbing. A big motivator for this different pursuit is its focus on the self. It's a sport that's all about pushing personal limits to the limit. You are given a wall, a cliff or a mountain and told to ascend to the summit or the top. When I'm climbing, I'm often pushing myself to a limit that no other sport does for me. There's a lot of metaphors with climbing and personal development and I think a great deal with climbing is the joy that comes with tackling the hardest problems you can put your body through and be able to better yourself with each route you top. The journey is very satisfying as a personal motivator. This has to lead me to challenge my body and to grow and build myself. This has given me the ability to tackle similar problems in life. There's a "let's try it out" mentality in rock climbing where I've learned to confidence to just "give things a shot". This mentality has given me a very colorful life, a life where I get to have unique experiences, that's made memories that I look back too. For example, a few weekends ago, a few of my friends just got on a bus at 2:30 am to Montreal alone and tried a university coding competition for fun. We actually won an award... that was meant for the students at McGill. It wasn't intentional, we came to this opportunity like "what happens if we ..." which ended up being a really cool experience. These are the philosophies I've learned while doing the things I loved and building on my body. The perspectives I've developed because of that.
