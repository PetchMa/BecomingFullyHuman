2020 March 21

Today was a strange day for me. I began developing this cough and this sore throat. Perhaps from this minor cold that my dad got a few days ago, but my parents are absolutely freaking out.

I suppose there is a reason for them to be carrying for me. As parents, I am their responsibility. and thus everything I do is by extension their responsibility. For example, the benefit of anything I do will never reflect on them, but all the consequences of my actions are absorbed by their responsibility.

That's why parents are so conservative. For example, when a kid climbs a tree, they don't experience the "joy" of climbing a tree. But in the case where the kid falls off a tree and breaks their leg, they absorb all of that responsibility. And thus parents are scared when their kid is in danger.

And so I've taught about this to myself. I always say that when I grow up, I will try my best to not repeat who my parents are. I believe that I can make the better parent but I honestly doubt I'd be any different than how I was raised.

This has always been a constant fear of mine. That I'll end up as the "monster I'm trying to defeat", as I've spoken to Mr. Struk before, this reminds me of the talk on the monolithic hero. The idea that we are all part of the same narrative.

This bias is a key contributor to my fear. That things aren't actually that different and that a part of my life is pre-destined in some sense.

The monolithic hero, operates in this awkward grey area where things aren't actually that concrete. It feels malleable and changeable.

Perhaps this is something I'll face when later on in life. Right now. I need to get better and less sick.
