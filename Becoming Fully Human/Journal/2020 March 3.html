Aleksander Solzhenitsyn was a decorated Russain Captain at the height of the Second World War. He fought courageously for his country during a time of need.

Unfortunately for Aleksander, no matter how noble his actions may have been, in 1945 he was exposed to have written private letters about Stalin criticizing the Soviet regime. Because of that he was punished as a threat to the state. He was later sent to a Soviet work camp, also known as a gulag.

Here Aleksander would spend the next 10 grueling years enslaved in arctic tundras of Siberia forced to work to his bones. What happened behind those iron curtains was perhaps the most brutal display of human evil possible.

The atrocities were so damn cruel that its very hard to think of any other point in history where there's been so much human suffering inflicted on so many people all at once.

It was estimated that 30 million people entered these labour camps and were faced with the worst of the worst. To give some context, prisoners were forced to work 14 hour shifts surviving on 140g of bread a day. They were starved to a point there eating rats, and insects in the prison became a viable option. Some, even ate each other. They worked through the cold to the point where people dropped dead. For those that died, prisoners would raid the corpse for any piece of clothing they can get their hands on. The people were forced to work so hard that prisoners would burn their own arms and mutilate their own body parts to avoid the labour. Female prisoners, even young kids, would trade sexual favours with other men simply to avoid getting raped by the prison guards.

The white sea baltic canal was an 80mile long channel of water that was built on top of hundreds of thousands of dead corpse. What was worse was that most of these prisoners were effectively innocent. One account from Aleksander Solzhenitsyn noted that 3 children were sentenced to these Gulags for knocking down a poster of Stalin. A carpenter was sentenced to 10 years for hanging his jacket on a sculpture of Lennin at home.

This was the closest humans came to the manifestation of true evil. A majority of these stories stem from Aleksander Solzhenitsyn books which detailed the unspeakable truths behind the Soviet Gulag system in 3 volumes spanning 2,000 pages.

Like anyone, after reading the account of the Soviet gulag I came face to face with the demon himself. Suffering. It surrounds us all.

Although today we might not have gone through the tragedies that occured behind the Gulags over 40 years ago, there has been by no means an end to our suffering.

This idea of appears to be ubiquitous throughout history. In fact over 2000 years ago a wise man named Siddhartha realized something similar as well.

However, one day Siddhartha went out onto the streets beyond his palace and witnessed what appears to be suffering. People were getting old, the old were getting sick and the sick were slowly dying. He eventually gave his teachings to fellow pupils and gave rise to what's known as Buddhism.

Buddhism is a religion that accepts the endless suffering of existence. Buddhism believes a person never actually dies. Whenever someone dies they are reincarnated again into another being. Buddhism believes that our suffering doesn‚Äôt only end at death but it exists in an infinite cycle known as the Wheel of Dharma.

Despite our impossibly gloomy fate, Siddhartha believes that we can transcend beyond this state to a place called Nirvana where we escape the endless suffering. The only way to do so is to follow what's called the 8 fold path.

Siddhartha believed that everything we do is in relation to Karma. There can be good or bad Karma depending on the intention of our actions. This suggests if we practice the actions of the 8 fold path we too can reach towards Nirvana or a state of higher being.

But what does this actually mean?

Right Understanding - Understanding that the Four Noble Truths are noble and true.

Right Thought- practice Buddhist faith.

Right Speech- Avoid cursing and slander and lying

Right conduct: refraining from any form of stealing or sexual impropriety.

Right means of making a living: Killing animals or working at jobs that force you to violate others.

Right mental attitude or effort: Avoiding negative thoughts and emotions
